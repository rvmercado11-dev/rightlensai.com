Current Focus

From Digital Safety to AI Readiness

Digital safety is part of a larger trust challenge for organizations adopting AI, automation, and digital platforms.

In that sense, digital safety is a shared responsibility - and AI readiness is one practical way organizations can operationalize it.

For Philippine SMEs and organizations, this means AI adoption should not only be fast or convenient. It should also be visible, accountable, safe, human-reviewed, and measurable.

RightLens AI helps translate digital safety into practical AI readiness by helping organizations answer seven management questions before scaling AI:

  1. What AI use case are we using?
  2. Who is accountable for the output?
  3. What data does the AI access?
  4. What data is off-limits?
  5. Where is human review required?
  6. What could go wrong, and how would we respond?
  7. How will we measure value and trust after 30 days?

Who This Is For

RightLens AI is for business owners, executives, managers, associations, and teams who are starting to use AI tools and want to avoid unclear ownership, unsafe data use, weak oversight, or unproven value.

We are especially focused on Philippine SMEs and organizations that want practical AI adoption - not abstract governance.

Practical Starting Point

Start with one visible AI use case.

Before scaling AI, clarify:

  • the business purpose
  • the accountable human owner
  • the data allowed and off-limits
  • the review and approval rules
  • the risks and response path
  • the 30-day value and trust measures

This is minimum viable AI governance in action.
